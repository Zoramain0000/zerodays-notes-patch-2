Guidelines & Encryption Info

⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING: If you forget your Master Password, your data is cryptographically impossible to recover. There are no backdoors.

enhanced_encryption Local Encryption

Zerodays Notes uses the Web Crypto API (AES-GCM 256-bit) to encrypt your notes locally.

  • Your Master Password is never stored. It is hashed using PBKDF2 (100,000 iterations) to derive the encryption key.
  • When saving, data is encrypted in-memory and written to IndexedDB as ciphertext.
  • When loading, data is decrypted only after you unlock the vault.
  • Images inside notes are also encrypted as Base64 ciphertext.

storage Data Storage & Export

All data lives strictly in your browser's IndexedDB (Local Storage). Nothing is sent to a server.

  • Export: Creates a .json file of your decrypted data. Keep this file safe! It is unencrypted plaintext.
  • Import: Merges a previously exported .json file into your current vault. The imported data will be encrypted with your current vault's key upon saving.

delete_sweep Wipe & Reset

If you forget your password or wish to start over, click "Wipe Vault & Reset" on the login screen. This will permanently delete the IndexedDB database and all stored salt/hashes.

sync Multi-Tab Sync

Uses the Broadcast Channel API. If you have the app open in two tabs, changes in one will automatically reflect in the other, provided both are unlocked.
